360° View of Sun Temple, Konark

Historical Background

The Sun Temple, Konark, a Heritage Site, is located in Odisha, India. Built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I, the temple is designed as a colossal chariot with intricately carved wheels, pillars, and walls. Dedicated to the Sun God, Surya, it is a masterpiece of Kalinga architecture and a symbol of India's rich cultural heritage.

Visitor Rules

  • Maintain silence and respect the sanctity of the site.
  • Photography is allowed but avoid using flash near delicate sculptures.
  • Do not touch or damage the carvings and sculptures.
  • Follow the instructions of the site staff.

Safety Precautions

  • Wear comfortable footwear as the site involves walking.
  • Carry water to stay hydrated during your visit.
  • Avoid visiting during peak hours to avoid crowds.
