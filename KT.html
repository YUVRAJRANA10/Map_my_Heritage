360° View of Khajuraho Temples

Historical Background

The Khajuraho Temples, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are located in Madhya Pradesh, India. Built between 950 and 1050 AD by the Chandela dynasty, these temples are renowned for their intricate sculptures and carvings depicting various aspects of life, spirituality, and art. The temples are divided into three groups: Western, Eastern, and Southern, with the Kandariya Mahadev Temple being the most iconic.

Visitor Rules

  • Maintain silence and respect the sanctity of the site.
  • Photography is allowed but avoid using flash near delicate sculptures.
  • Do not touch or damage the carvings and sculptures.
  • Follow the instructions of the site staff.

Safety Precautions

  • Wear comfortable footwear as the site involves walking.
  • Carry water to stay hydrated during your visit.
  • Avoid visiting during peak hours to avoid crowds.
