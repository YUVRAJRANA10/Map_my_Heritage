360° View of Great Living Chola Temples

Historical Background

The Great Living Chola Temples, built during the Chola dynasty, are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. These temples, including the Brihadeeswarar Temple, Gangaikonda Cholapuram, and Airavatesvara Temple, showcase the architectural brilliance and cultural heritage of the Chola Empire. They are a testament to the artistic and engineering skills of ancient India.

Visitor Rules

  • Maintain silence inside the temple premises to respect its sanctity.
  • Photography is allowed only in designated areas.
  • Do not touch or damage the sculptures and carvings.
  • Follow the instructions of the temple staff.

Safety Precautions

  • Wear comfortable footwear as the temple complex involves walking.
  • Carry water to stay hydrated during your visit.
  • Avoid visiting during peak hours to avoid crowds.
