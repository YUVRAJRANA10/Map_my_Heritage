Historical Background

Amber Fort, also known as Amer Fort, is a majestic fortress located in Amer, Rajasthan, India, about 11 kilometers from Jaipur. Built in 1592 by Raja Man Singh I, a trusted general of Mughal Emperor Akbar, the fort served as the main residence of the Rajput Maharajas. Blending Hindu and Mughal architectural styles, the fort is renowned for its artistic elements like ornate mirror work, intricate carvings, and grand courtyards. It was later expanded by successive rulers, especially Jai Singh I. Set atop a hill, the fort overlooks Maota Lake and stands as a symbol of Rajasthan's royal heritage.

Visitor Rules

  • Photography is allowed, but no tripods or drones.
  • Do not bring outside food or drinks.
  • Don’t touch or damage historical structures.
  • Stick to designated areas; don’t enter restricted zones

Safety Precautions

  • Protect yourself from the sun with hats, sunscreen, and water.
  • Beware of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Keep distance from monkeys and wildlife.
  • Avoid unofficial guides or aggressive vendors.
