360° View of Rani ki Vav

Historical Background

Rani ki Vav, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is located in Patan, Gujarat, India. Built in the 11th century by Queen Udayamati in memory of her husband, King Bhimdev I, this stepwell is an architectural marvel. It features intricate carvings and sculptures depicting mythological and religious themes, showcasing the rich heritage of ancient India.

Visitor Rules

  • Maintain silence and respect the sanctity of the site.
  • Photography is allowed but avoid using flash near delicate sculptures.
  • Do not touch or damage the carvings and sculptures.
  • Follow the instructions of the site staff.

Safety Precautions

  • Wear comfortable footwear as the site involves walking and climbing stairs.
  • Carry water to stay hydrated during your visit.
  • Avoid visiting during peak hours to avoid crowds.
